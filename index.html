What VerveMD Does

Do NOT call

03

Your Script

Before you dial

Know the practice name, the doctor's name if possible, the specialty, and the city. That's it. You don't need to research deeply. The script is designed to work cold.

Step 1 · Getting Past the Front Desk Use when a receptionist or staff member answers
You
"Hey, how are you doing today?"
Note
Wait for a response. Be warm, not rushed. One second of genuine interaction matters.
You
"I'm calling on behalf of Chris Cook over at VerveMD. He works specifically with longevity and concierge practices on AI search visibility. Who would be the right person to connect with about the practice's marketing?"
Them
[They give you a name or transfer you, or say "that's the doctor" / "that's Dr. ___"]
You
"Great, is [name] available for just a quick moment?"
Note
If they say the doctor is with a patient or unavailable, ask: "No problem. What's the best time to reach them?" and note it down. Don't leave a generic voicemail unless you have to.
Step 2 · The Main Call (Decision Maker) Use when you reach the doctor, owner, or office manager
You
"Hey [name], how are you doing today?"
Note
Wait. Let them respond. Don't rush into the pitch.
You
"I'll be quick. I'm calling on behalf of Chris Cook at VerveMD. We work specifically with longevity and concierge practices. Quick question. Do you know if your practice shows up when someone searches for [their specialty] in [their city] on ChatGPT or Perplexity?"
Note
This is the key question. Most people will say "no" or "I have no idea." That's the door. Stay quiet for a moment after you ask it. Let the question land.
Them
"No, I'm not sure." / "I don't think so." / "What do you mean?"
You
"That's exactly why I'm calling. Most practices in your category aren't showing up there yet, and three practices end up dominating any given market in AI search. It shifts fast. Chris does a free AI Visibility Audit. It shows exactly where your practice stands, takes a day to turn around, no obligation. Would it make sense to get that in front of you?"
Them
[If yes] "Sure, okay." / "What do you need?"
You
"Perfect. I just need your practice website URL and the city you primarily serve. Chris will run the audit and send it over directly. What's the best email to send it to?"
Note
Get: website URL, primary city, best email. That's a win. Log it and pass to Chris.
Voicemail Script Use only if you've tried twice and can't reach anyone live
You
"Hi, this is Samalee calling on behalf of Chris Cook at VerveMD. We work specifically with longevity and concierge practices on AI search visibility, making sure practices show up when patients search on ChatGPT or Perplexity. If that's something worth a look, Chris does a free audit, no obligation. You can reach me at [your number] or email Chris directly at chris@vervemd.com. Thanks."
Note
Keep it under 25 seconds. Don't sound like you're reading. Voicemails rarely convert. Focus energy on live conversations.
04

Objections and Responses

Remember

You are not selling anything. You are offering a free audit. Most objections soften quickly when you remind them of that. Your job is to keep the door open, not to win an argument.

They say "We already have a marketing company."
"Good. This is different. It's specifically about AI search, which is a separate problem from SEO or paid ads. Most agencies aren't tracking it yet. The audit just shows you what's there. Worth knowing either way."
Why it works: you're not attacking their current agency. You're filling a gap they probably don't know exists.
They say "Not interested."
"Understood. Can I ask, is it the timing or just not on the radar right now?" [If timing] "When would be better to follow up?" [If not a priority] "Got it. I'll make a note. Thanks for your time."
Why it works: distinguishes between a timing issue and a real no. A lot of "not interested" is really "not right now."
They say "Can you just send me some information?"
"I'd rather send the actual audit. It's specific to your practice and a lot more useful than a brochure. Chris just needs your website URL and the city you primarily serve. Can I grab those real quick?"
Why it works: redirects to the ask. The audit is the information, and it's personalized, which makes it harder to ignore.
They say "We're not looking to spend more on marketing."
"The audit is completely free. No obligation, no pitch. It just shows where you stand in AI search right now. You can decide what to do with the information. Would that be worth a look?"
Why it works: removes the cost objection entirely by leading with free.
They say "We're fully booked already."
"That's great. So it's more about keeping that consistent, right? AI search visibility is actually where that starts to matter most. It's the patients who are actively looking and don't have a referral yet. The audit just shows you what's happening there. Free to take a look."
Why it works: being booked doesn't mean the problem doesn't exist. It reframes to retention and consistency.
They say "Who are you again?" / "What company is this?"
"VerveMD. We work specifically with longevity and concierge practices on AI search visibility. Making sure practices show up when patients search on ChatGPT or Perplexity for their specialty in their market."
Why it works: clean, specific, re-orients without getting defensive.
They say "I need to talk to the doctor / owner first."
"Completely understand. What's the best way to get a note in front of them? Should I call back at a specific time, or is there an email I can send a quick summary to?"
Why it works: keeps momentum. Gets you a name, time, or email instead of a dead end.
05

What to Log After Each Call

After every call, even a hang-up, log these five things so Chris can track and follow up.

  1. Practice name and phone number
    The business name as it appears on their website or when they answer the phone.
  2. Who you spoke to (or reached)
    Name and role if they gave it: "front desk," "office manager Jane," "Dr. Patel directly."
  3. Outcome
    One of: Audit requested (WIN, get website + email), Call back scheduled, Not interested, No answer, Left voicemail, Gatekeeper only.
  4. If audit requested: website URL, city, best email
    These three pieces are what Chris needs to run the audit. Grab all three before you hang up.
  5. Any notes on objections or tone
    "Friendly but busy," "said call back Friday," "already working with an SEO company," "seemed interested but deferred to doctor." Anything useful for follow-up.
06

Rules to Remember

Always Lead with the free audit. Never a sales call.
Your CTA is always: "Chris does a free audit, no obligation." Never "can I schedule a call to pitch you." The audit is what you're booking.
Always Ask the qualifying question every time.
"Do you know if your practice shows up on ChatGPT or Perplexity?" This is the hook. Ask it on every call. Most won't know the answer, and that creates the conversation.
Never Quote pricing.
If they ask what it costs, say: "The audit is free. If it shows something worth addressing, Chris will walk through what that looks like, but he doesn't pitch until after the audit." That's it.
Never Try to explain the full service.
You don't need to explain AEO, SEO, AI visibility, or the inquiry system in detail. The simpler it sounds, the better. "AI search visibility" is enough. Keep it short.
Never Argue or push hard on a firm no.
If someone says no twice, thank them and move on. We're not grinding people. A graceful exit leaves the door open for a future call.
The only metric that matters

Audits requested per day. If you get two to three people to give you their website URL and email address, that's a great day. Volume matters. 20 to 30 dials a day is a realistic target once you're in a rhythm.
